Horror gripped children sit

in despairs deepest pit.
You should feel the same.
In the air, guilt and shame
hang heavy over bony shoulders.
Fear of damnation smoulders.
(Fired by a doctrine old)
At six years old
can evil take hold?

The graphic revelations,
the moral obligations,
(The beauty and the wonder
in all of God’s creations)
young minds comprehend.
But how could the Lord intend
That parents not be told
What happens when the cold,
fervent swinging of an arm
inflicts such harm?

Divine secrets between pupil and teacher,
Children cower when in comes the preacher.
So instead, pugilism in playgrounds,
a primal scream resounds
Beneath the sneering features
of self-righteous teachers.

I would have them punished.
Wouldn’t they be astonished
to see a man twenty years late
stood behind as they wait.
Clenched fists and rage,
held tight in a moral cage.
